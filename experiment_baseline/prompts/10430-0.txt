\[
\logDeriv (x) = \frac{1}{x}.
\]
Substituting this into our previous equation, we get:
\[
\logDeriv (x^n) = n \cdot \frac{1}{x} = \frac{n}{x}.
\]
Thus, the logarithmic derivative of \( x^n \) at \( x \) is indeed \( \frac{n}{x} \). This completes the proof. \(\blacksquare\)
